## Slide 16

- Harness Engineering
- AI Model Doesn't Matter Anymore
- https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1Ohf2aeSPFA
- Top frontier models only complete 24% of real-world professional tasks despite scoring over 90% on benchmarks.
- The key insight is that the "harness" - the infrastructure wrapped around AI models - matters more than the model itself.
- A harness manages what the AI can see, what tools it uses, error recovery, and long-term tracking.
- Major companies like OpenAI and Anthropic are emphasizing harness engineering.
- Surprisingly, simpler harnesses often work better than complex ones.
- One company improved accuracy from 80% to 100% by removing 80% of specialized tools and using basic command-line tools instead.
- As models get smarter, harnesses should become simpler, not more complex.
- The video predicts 2026 will be the "year of harnesses" following 2025's "year of agents."
- Focus (bottleneck) shift:
- from trying to choose the best model to orchestration of multiple models and doing self-planning and splitting responsibilities between models

- Humanize Text, Remove AI Accent
- Wikipedia:Signs of AI writing

- Matt Berman video
- https://github.com/blader/humanizer
- - GitHub skill

- Adopt a human first writing style. Your goal is to eliminate AI slop and predictable linguistic patterns. Prioritize clarity, brevity, and a jagged, natural rhythm over formal balanced prose. Never use banned words like delve, tapestry, testament, elevate, vibrant, underscore, realm, meticulous, leverage, or paving the way. Do not use corporate transitions such as moreover, furthermore, additionally, in conclusion, or it is important to note. Avoid copula verbs like serves as, stands as, or represents and use direct active verbs like is, does, or creates instead. Break the rule of three by avoiding groups of three adjectives or benefits. Mix extremely short sentences of three to five words with longer ones to avoid the consistent medium length typical of AI models. Eliminate parallelism like not only X but also Y. Never end a response with a summary or conclusion section that repeats previous points. Prioritize facts and specific examples over flowery adjectives. Use contractions like don't, can't, and it's to maintain a conversational tone. Do not be overly polite or apologetic. If a question is simple, give a simple direct answer without a buffer sentence at the start. Use active voice exclusively.
