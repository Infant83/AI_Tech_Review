# Deep Research Prompt v1

## Topic
- `Blue PHOLED IP + simulation stack`
- Date sensitivity: `as of 2026-04-10 Asia/Seoul`

## Audience
- OLED materials researchers
- display device engineers
- technical managers evaluating blue PHOLED roadmap credibility

## Research Objective
Produce a technically rigorous report that separates what is publicly verifiable from what is still inference in the recent `March-April 2026` blue-PHOLED-adjacent signal cluster surfaced by GPT Pulse.

## Core Questions
1. What exactly is publicly verifiable in the latest UDC patent and press-release activity around emissive-layer materials, organometallic ligands, host mixtures, and PHOLED architecture?
2. Which public UDC artifacts are directly blue-PHOLED-specific, and which are broader platform or portfolio signals?
3. What does the newly granted UDC single-crucible carbazole/triazine host-mixture patent actually claim, and why does it matter for manufacturability or stack design?
4. What does the relevant Idemitsu long-lifetime host / multi-emissive-layer patent signal about the competitive landscape?
5. What is genuinely new in `2026` versus already-established OLED host/emitter design practice?
6. What would a real multiscale simulation stack for blue PHOLED lifetime prediction look like?
7. Which public tools are useful for each layer of that stack, and where do `TMMax`, `TMM-Fast`, `grcwa`, and QUBO-style optimization actually fit?
8. What experimental validation loop would be required to connect simulation to device-level lifetime claims credibly?

## Required Source Priorities
1. Primary sources first:
   - UDC press releases
   - UDC and Idemitsu patent records
   - official docs / repos for simulation tools
2. Then technical literature:
   - peer-reviewed papers or recognized preprints for blue PHOLED lifetime, host design, and simulation methods
3. Use secondary summaries only to fill gaps, and clearly label them as secondary.

## Required Deliverables
- An executive summary that distinguishes:
  - verified facts
  - strong inferences
  - unresolved questions
- A UDC vs Idemitsu comparison focused on:
  - claim scope
  - host strategy
  - stack / process implications
  - likely commercialization significance
- A simulation-stack map from:
  - quantum chemistry / descriptor stage
  - exciton / degradation stage
  - optical stack stage
  - experimental validation stage
- A section on what Pulse got right, what it compressed too aggressively, and what remains uncertain.
- A final view on whether the signal is best interpreted as:
  - molecule-level breakthrough
  - process integration move
  - architecture-readiness move
  - or a combination of the above

## Explicit Comparison Points
- UDC one-crucible host-mixture strategy vs Idemitsu multi-emissive-layer / host-energy-alignment strategy
- Broad PHOLED platform messaging vs explicit blue-device evidence
- Optical simulation tooling vs actual lifetime-prediction tooling
- QUBO-style optimization as exploratory screening vs experimentally validated design guidance

## Risks / Uncertainty To Validate
- Do not overstate that a public UDC patent equals `commercial blue PHOLED readiness`.
- Do not equate TMM/RCWA acceleration with full lifetime prediction.
- Do not assume Pulse's `2026년 3월 이데미츠 특허` phrasing maps cleanly to one exact public record without verifying the document trail.
- Flag where public data stops and expert inference begins.

## Output Style
- Write for expert readers, not for a general audience.
- Prefer exact dates, patent numbers, and source URLs.
- Use tables or structured comparisons where helpful.
- Include concrete next-step experiments or validation work.
